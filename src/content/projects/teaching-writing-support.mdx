## Overview

Students rarely arrive with a generic "writing problem." They arrive with mismatched assumptions, unclear assignments, uneven confidence, and real constraints on time and attention. Good writing support has to diagnose those conditions before it can offer useful advice.

This body of work centers that diagnostic task. It treats teaching support as situated problem-solving rather than as the delivery of canned study skills. The work ranges from one-on-one writing support to large-course teaching assistance, but the principle is the same in each setting: find the real bottleneck, then help the student build a path through it.

## Role

Reese worked at the level where pedagogy becomes practical: helping students revise, interpret prompts, structure arguments, and recover momentum when learning gets stuck. At NC State, that has meant graduate writing consultations and repeated teaching support in an 80-student U.S. Foreign Policy course. The role required close listening, clear explanation, and a willingness to tailor support to the real shape of the problem.

## What I did

The method was student-centered in a precise sense. Instead of beginning from an ideal paper or a fixed template, Reese looked for the actual bottleneck. Was the problem conceptual? Structural? Emotional? Linguistic? Was the student struggling with evidence, with audience, or with the terms of the assignment itself?

In practice, that meant becoming the first point of contact for students in PS 331, helping them navigate research assignments, hosting group tutoring sessions, and using non-directive methods that aimed for independent writers rather than quick paper fixes. It also meant learning how to operate fairly inside a politically diverse classroom without passing judgment, so students could ask difficult questions and still feel safe enough to learn.

That same logic extends to larger instructional questions, including the growing role of AI in student learning. The important question is not whether tools exist, but what kinds of thinking they encourage or short-circuit. Good support work names that difference and helps students build durable habits rather than dependency.

## Why it matters

The most important outcome of this work is not a single finished artifact. It is a repeatable practice of helping people move from uncertainty to agency. That practice matters in classrooms, in writing centers, and anywhere explanation has to meet a real person at the point of need.

As a portfolio case study, this page shows that Reese's teaching identity is not abstract. It is grounded in recurring learning problems, large-class logistics, writing-center work, and practical interventions that make progress possible. It also reflects formal pedagogical development, including CRLA Level III certification in writing tutoring.

## Skills

- Diagnosing learning problems at the level of structure, not just surface error
- Explaining writing and revision in ways students can act on immediately
- Adapting support across different learners, assignments, and institutional contexts
- Bringing critical judgment to the question of AI in learning environments
